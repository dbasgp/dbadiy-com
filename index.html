Step 01 of 07

Preparation

Before installation, bench-test the unit on the floor. Confirm the dehumidifier runs normally.

10-min bench test Hatch 450 / 600 mm Service panel orientation
Compulsory

10-minute bench test, before you hang it

After unboxing, place the dehumidifier on the floor or a workbench and run a 10-minute power-on check. (The unit must be powered on for testing both before and after installation.)

  1. Unbox — open the carton and lay the dehumidifier flat on the floor. Keep the packaging in case you need to return it.
  2. LCD control panel — connect the LCD control panel signal cable to terminals 4-7 on the unit.
  3. Temperature & humidity sensor — connect the temperature & humidity sensor to terminals 1-3 on the unit.
  4. Power up — a grounded 220-240V socket can be used temporarily.
  5. Switch on — the fan starts immediately; the compressor follows after a 3-second delay (make sure the unit is in dehumidification mode).
  6. Force the compressor on — set the humidity target to 40% RH or lower. (If the target is above ambient RH, the compressor will not start.)
  7. Run for more than 10 minutes in dehumidification mode — listen for any unusual noise from the fan or compressor. Warm air at the outlet means dehumidification is working. You can also hold the temperature & humidity sensor near the outlet — the RH shown on the LCD control panel will gradually drop. There is a metal fan inside the outlet — never put your hands or any object into it.
  8. Check the LCD control panel — confirm the LCD control panel display and controls work normally, with no fault code shown.
No error code? You can start the install.

Before you start, confirm:

  • Confirm the plenum height — UTC20 needs 200mm, UTC68 needs 240mm, UTC120 needs 310mm — and leave 2-30mm of clearance above and below, so the body never touches the slab or the false ceiling.
  • Confirm the hanging position — mark where the four threaded rods will be fixed into the slab, avoiding existing services inside the slab.
  • Pick the hatch location — a hatch of 450 × 450 mm or 600 × 600 mm is recommended, directly in front of the service-panel side (the drainage outlet side).
  • Plan duct routes early — sketch the return and supply paths, mark every elbow and count them. Each 90° bend costs about 10 Pa.

Step 02 of 07

Hanging the unit

Four threaded rods, levelled body, rubber washers between the rod and the foot. Get this right and you'll never hear the unit running.

4 × M10 304 SS rods ≥ 2 cm rod spare Rubber vibration washers

How to hang it

  1. 1
    Drop four threaded rods from the slab anchors, M10 in 304 stainless. Cut them so at least 2 cm sticks out below the unit foot — that gives you room to level later.
  2. 2
    Slide a rubber washer onto each rod before the foot, and another one above the nut. The washers are what stop fan vibration travelling into the slab.
  3. 3
    Lift the unit onto the rods through the four positioning holes (numbered 1–4 on the chassis).
  4. 4
    Level it horizontally with a spirit level on the top face. The drain pan inside has to sit flat or condensate pools in the wrong corner.
  5. 5
    Tighten the nuts evenly, diagonal pattern, until the rubber compresses lightly — not flat.

Orientation — get this right before drilling

The unit is not symmetrical. Each side does a different job. Decide the orientation before you drill the slab anchors, because it dictates where the access hatch, duct openings, and drain route need to be.

  • Service / access panel side — must face the planned false-ceiling hatch. The filter slides out from this side and the electrical compartment opens from here. Never face it to a wall — you will lose all service access.
  • Air inlet vs air outlet — opposite ends of the body. Plan the return and supply duct routes around this from the start.
  • Pump drainage outlet — on one of the long sides. The drain route needs clear access on that side.
  • Wired control box & rear panel — the status LED, emergency-stop button, and power cord outlet are on the rear. Don't bury that face against a structure.

Access hatch & service clearance

The official manual is specific: the access hatch must sit directly in front of the service panel side (the drainage outlet side). No offset, no compromises — service techs need to slide the filter out and reach the drain.

  • Hatch size: 450 × 450 mm or 600 × 600 mm, square, directly in front of the service panel. 600 is more comfortable for service.
  • ≥ 450 mm clearance around the unit inside the false-ceiling void — room to remove side covers and reach the drain.
  • ≥ 10 mm air gap between the unit body and the slab above, and between the unit and the false ceiling below.
  • Maintenance every 3–6 months — filter, coil inspection, drain check — so the hatch will get used. Make it useable.
If the service panel ends up facing a wall, the install is wrong. You cannot change the filter, open the access panel, or service the drain without dropping the whole unit. Catch this before tightening the suspension rods.
Don't let the body touch the slab. Even a couple of millimetres of contact will transmit fan vibration straight into the structure above. The air gap is the silencer.

Step 03 of 07

Drainage

Every UTC ships with a built-in pump that lifts condensate up to 1.8 m, with a check valve already integrated inside the unit. You just need to add the Upper U-trap and route the pipe.

1.8 m pump lift Check valve built-in Add Upper U-trap

The drain path

  1. 1
    The check valve is built into the unit — no need to fit one externally. It prevents back-siphon when the pump cycles off.
  2. 2
    Fit an Upper U-trap on the outlet line for gas isolation, as shown on the official install card. This blocks drain-line air and odours from coming back into the plenum.
  3. 3
    Insulated drain hose from the outlet to the nearest stack. Vertical lift first (up to 1.8 m), then horizontal fall to the drain.
  4. 4
    Wrap the pipe across the plenum — it carries cold condensate through warm humid air and will sweat onto the ceiling otherwise.
  5. 5
    Two units sharing a route? Drop each one separately and merge low. Never tee them at the top.

How the pump cycles

While the unit runs, the built-in pump runs 3 minutes on, 5 minutes off, repeating. If the drain backs up, fault code E3 (water full protection) appears on the LCD.

Before you sign off

Pour a litre of water slowly into the drain pan through the service hatch. The pump should kick in within a minute and clear the pan. Watch every joint on the route for the next five minutes — that's when a bad seal will start dripping.

Step 04 of 07

Ducting

Flexible aluminium foil for most runs; rigid galvanised for longer or stiffer sections. Always join the unit flange to the rigid duct via a 300–500 mm flexible section — that's what stops compressor vibration travelling into the rooms.

300–500 mm flex section Total main duct ≤ 10 m Supply 1.5 – 3 m/s

Manufacturer's rules

  • Flexible aluminium foil duct is the default — lightweight, easy, quiet. Use rigid galvanised for long or stiff runs.
  • Total main duct length (supply + return) ≤ 10 m. Beyond that, airflow drops and the unit labours.
  • Maximum 5 supply vents and 5 return vents. Branch ducts ≥ 75 mm diameter.
  • 300–500 mm flexible section at the unit's flanges — vibration isolation. Never connect rigid duct straight to the flange.
  • 1 m of straight duct before the first branch. Avoid sharp bends right after the unit.
  • Volume damper on every supply vent — balance branches so the pressure difference is ≤ 15%.
  • ≥ 10 mm clearance from ceiling slab and false ceiling. The body must never touch structure.
  • Supply outlet velocity 1.5 – 3 m/s — below is sluggish, above whistles.

Common layouts

1

Standalone

Return and supply both go to the room directly. Simplest install, fully independent of any HVAC. Default for most apartments.

2

Return-side injection

Dehumidifier sits in parallel with the HVAC return. Dries the air before it hits the cooling coil — coil sweats less, latent load drops.

3

Supply-side injection

Pulls separately from the room, discharges into the HVAC supply. Needs a backdraft damper so HVAC pressure can't push back.

4

Bypass loop

Return from the HVAC return, supply into the HVAC supply. Full parallel loop — works when you want dedicated humidity control alongside cooling.

5

Standalone + fresh air

Adds an outdoor branch into the return side. Dries make-up air on the way in. Useful in sealed apartments where CO₂ climbs overnight.

6

Multi-zone manifold

One unit, central plenum, branches to every room. Whole-house drying with a UTC68 or UTC120. Add backdraft dampers on each branch.

Skip the flexible section and you'll regret it. The 300–500 mm flex piece between the unit flange and the rigid duct is what absorbs compressor vibration. Without it, every vent in the apartment will hum.

Step 05 of 07

Controls

The LCD wall panel ships with 8 m of cable and fits a standard 86-type back box. Signal wires are sequenced — connect them in order, with the power off.

86 × 86 mm back box 8 m cable supplied 13 wired terminals

Where to mount the panel

  • Common wall, line of sight — somewhere everyone in the room will actually see it.
  • 1.3 – 1.5 m above the floor. Tall enough to read, low enough to reach.
  • Away from doors and air paths. A panel in the supply airstream reads dry air and shuts the unit off prematurely.
  • 86 × 86 mm back box, sunk flush. Run the 8 m cable through conduit, never loose in the void.

Wired control terminals

Behind the side cover. Terminals 1–7 carry low-voltage signals out to the sensor and control panel. Terminals 8–13 are passive dry contact only — never apply external voltage, it will back-feed and damage the mainboard.

1 – 3
Temperature & humidity sensor GND · +5V · DATA. The supplied 5 m sensor cable plugs in here. The unit will not enter dehumidification mode without a valid reading.
4 – 7
Wired control panel RX · TX · +5V · GND. The supplied 8 m LCD panel cable plugs in here. Panel sits in a standard 86 × 86 mm back box.
8 – 9
Remote on/off (dry contact) Short the two terminals via any unpowered switch — BMS interlock, fire-alarm shutdown, push-button. NO (default): short = on, open = off. NC: short = off, open = on (configurable on the panel).
10 – 11
Water leak sensor Connect the two black sensing wires of a standard leak-detection cable. Moisture anywhere along the cable triggers fault code E7 and an auto-shutdown.
12 – 13
Reserved For future expansion — do not connect without manufacturer instructions.

RS485 & WiFi

  • RS485 Modbus — A/B ports behind the LCD panel, next to GND/+5V/TX/RX. 9600 bps. Standard Modbus RTU register map for BMS, HMI, PLC.
  • WiFi via Tuya Smart app — phone on 2.4 GHz WiFi (not 5 GHz). Hold the fan-speed button on the panel until the WiFi indicator flashes, then add the device from the app.
  • Status indicator LED + emergency stop button on the rear of the unit, next to the power cord outlet — for local diagnostics and a quick local kill switch.
Never feed voltage into terminals 8–13. These are passive dry-contact inputs. Any external power source — even 5 V — will back-feed into the mainboard and kill the controller. Use only unpowered switches, probes, or sensing cables.
Power off before you touch any signal wire. Plugging or unplugging the control panel while live will fry the control board. Per the install guide: power down, connect, then power up.

Step 06 of 07

Power

One dedicated circuit, properly earthed. Don't share it with anything else — compressor startup current is high enough to nuisance-trip a shared breaker.

3 × 2.5 mm² minimum 16 A dedicated breaker Proper earth

Wiring

  • Cable: 3 × 2.5 mm² minimum from the consumer unit to the unit's terminal block. Larger if the run is over 15 m.
  • Breaker: dedicated 16 A air switch, type C MCB. RCBO if local code requires it.
  • Earth: connected to the unit's earth terminal — not the chassis screw. Verify continuity with a meter.
  • Isolator: reachable from the hatch, so service techs can lock-off without going back to the DB.

Order of operations

  1. 1
    Run the cable from the DB to the unit, through proper conduit. Don't loop excess inside the unit — coil it cleanly outside.
  2. 2
    Land the cores at the terminal block. Live, neutral, earth — to the labelled terminals only. Torque them properly.
  3. 3
    Connect signal wires next, with the breaker still off. They're numbered and sequenced — match labels exactly.
  4. 4
    Replace the access panel before re-energising. The interlock is what protects the next person.
  5. 5
    Energise from the DB, watch the panel come up, then run the commissioning check.
Never work the unit live. The compressor terminals carry several hundred volts during start, and the capacitor stays charged after the breaker trips. Lock off, verify dead, then open.

Step 07 of 07

Commissioning

Ten minutes of watching the unit run is what separates a clean install from a callback in six weeks.

10 min watch test No fault codes Drain leak test

The 10-minute test

  1. 1
    Energise. LCD wall panel boots — no error code.
  2. 2
    Set to dehumidification mode with target 50% RH. Fan should ramp cleanly through low → medium → high.
  3. 3
    Compressor kicks in within 90 seconds. No knocking, no metallic rattle, no surging fan note.
  4. 4
    Listen at the supply grille. No whistle, no fluttering damper. If there's a whistle, the duct is undersized.
  5. 5
    Watch the drain. Within five minutes the pump should cycle once. Check every joint on the route, then the U-trap.
  6. 6
    After 10 minutes, humidity reading on the panel should be trending down. If it's flat or rising, recheck airflow before you blame the unit.

Hand-off

Show the homeowner three things, in this order: filter access (and how to wash it), how to change setpoint on the panel, how to pair the WiFi app. Leave them the install card and the user manual. Note the unit serial on the wall behind the panel — it's the first thing service will ask for.

That's a clean install. A properly commissioned UTC will run quietly for a decade with monthly filter rinses and a yearly coil wipe.

If something feels off

Troubleshooting

Most "broken" dehumidifiers are starved-airflow units, full filters, or stalled drains. Walk through these before calling service.

Output low Not pulling out as much water as it used to
  1. Pull the filter — if it's grey or matted, rinse under warm water, air dry, refit. Try again for 24 hours.
  2. Check airflow at the supply grille with the back of your hand. Should feel firm. If it's weak, ducting may have collapsed or kinked.
  3. Check the coil through the hatch. Frost on the coil at room temp = low refrigerant — call service.
  4. Confirm the room isn't being constantly re-humidified — open window, wet laundry, plants.
Noise Rattling, knocking, or humming through the ceiling
  1. Open the hatch and check whether the unit body is touching the slab. If yes, slacken the rod nuts a turn — the rubber washers do the isolating.
  2. Check the duct connections. Loose flex against a metal collar buzzes — re-clip and tape.
  3. Knocking on startup is the compressor. Persistent knocking once running = mounting bolts loose or refrigerant charge issue.
Water Dripping from the ceiling or hatch
  1. Power down first.
  2. Open the hatch. If water is in the drain pan, the pump's not clearing — check the check valve and the U-trap.
  3. If water is on top of the supply duct, the duct is sweating — insulate it across the plenum.
  4. If water is at a joint, reseal with mastic and re-clip.
Panel Fault code on the LCD

From the official manual. Power-cycle the breaker for 60 seconds before assuming any code is real.

  • E1 — Evaporator coil temperature sensor fault. Unit falls back to timed defrost. Reseat the sensor connection; if persists, call service.
  • E2 — Temperature & humidity sensor fault. The unit cannot regulate without it. Reseat the 5 m sensor cable at terminals 1–3.
  • E3 — Water full protection. The drain pan or pump is not clearing. Check the pump outlet, U-trap, and pipe slope.
  • E4 — Low refrigerant pressure protection. Call service — do not reset repeatedly.
  • E7 — Water leak sensor triggered. Find and dry the leak, then the code clears.
  • E8 — High exhaust temperature protection. Usually airflow restriction — check filter and ducting.
  • E9 — Discharge temperature sensor fault. Call service.

If a code persists after a full power cycle, call service with the code and the unit's factory serial number.

No power Panel dark, unit unresponsive
  1. Check the breaker at the DB. If it has tripped, reset once. If it trips again, stop — there's a real fault.
  2. If the breaker holds, check the isolator at the hatch.
  3. If both are on but the panel is dark, the panel cable may have loosened — power down, reseat, retry.
Smell Musty smell from the supply grille
  1. Filter — rinse it. This is the cause 80% of the time.
  2. Run the UV-C lamp continuously for a few cycles (selectable from the panel).
  3. If it persists, schedule a yearly coil wipe-down — the coil itself can grow biofilm over time.

A clean unit lasts a decade

Maintenance

Three jobs across the year. None of them takes more than ten minutes.

Every 2 weeks

Clean the filter

Per the manual: check the filter at least every two weeks. Open the hatch, slide the mesh filter out. Gently tap or vacuum to remove dust, or wash in warm water (≤ 40 °C) with a little neutral detergent, rinse clean, air-dry completely before refitting.

  • In dusty environments: check weekly, or daily if needed.
  • Don't expose the filter to direct sunlight or heat sources — it will warp.
  • Never refit a damp filter — biofilm starts on day one, and airflow drops.
Quarterly

Eyes-on check

Five minutes through the hatch. You're looking for things that have moved or changed since the install.

  • Drain pipe joints — any sign of dampness or mineral crust?
  • Insulation on the supply duct — still intact and dry?
  • Rod nuts — finger-test for any that have loosened.
  • Panel humidity reading vs. a separate hygrometer in the room — should agree within 3%.
Yearly

Coil & drain service

Power off at the breaker first. Wipe the casing with a soft, clean, dry cloth — or a cloth lightly dampened with mild detergent for stubborn soiling, then wipe clean with a plain damp cloth. Never rinse or wash the unit with running water — it will cause insulation failure and risk of shock. Run a litre of water through the drain pan to confirm the pump still clears quickly.

  • Don't use solvents, abrasive powders, or polishing agents — they damage the surface.
  • Wipe the coil fins with a soft brush only. Never bend the fins.
  • If the unit has a UV-C lamp, replace every 18 – 24 months.
If you live above a kitchen or near the sea, halve every interval. Cooking oil and salt aerosol coat the coil twice as fast.
